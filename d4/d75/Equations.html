It is possible to recognize 7 non-dimensional numbers:

  • Strouhal number; it is the ratio between characteristic frequency and the fluid dynamic one; for non periodic flow it is set to 1;
  • Ruark number; it is the ratio between inertial (convective) force and pressure one; for a Newtonian fluid it is set to 1;
  • Mach number; it is the ratio between velocity and speed of sound;
  • Reynolds number; it is the ratio between inertial (convective) force and viscous one;
  • Froude number; it is the ratio between inertial (convective) force and volume (mass) one;
  • Prandtl number; it is the ratio between momentum diffusion and heat one;
  • Eckert number; it is the ratio between kinetic energy and enthalpy one;

For the convective fluxes Riemann Problem solvers are used (see Riemann module library). High order approximation of numerical fluxes is obtained by means of WENO (Weighted Essentially Non Oscillatory) reconstruction, see WENO module library.

For the diffusive fluxes a standard central second order approximation is used (based on Finite-Volume derivative approximation).

The time integration of the unsteady terms is computed by means of Runge-Kutta schemes, see Runge-Kutta module library.
